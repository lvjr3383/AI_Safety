Specification for "Process Payments Securely":

Core Behavior:
- The system must facilitate the processing of payments between a payer and a payee.
- It must ensure that all payment transactions are executed securely, maintaining the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of payment data.
- The system should support various payment methods, including credit/debit cards, bank transfers, and digital wallets.

Inputs:
- Payment details, including payer and payee information, payment amount, and currency.
- Payment method information, such as credit card number, expiration date, CVV, bank account details, or digital wallet credentials.
- Authentication credentials for the payer, such as username/password or biometric data.
- Optional: Promotional codes or discounts that may apply to the transaction.

Outputs:
- Confirmation of successful payment processing, including a transaction ID and receipt.
- Error messages in case of failed transactions, specifying the reason for failure.
- Notifications to both payer and payee regarding the transaction status.

Assumptions:
- The system will use a third-party payment gateway for processing transactions.
- Payment data will be encrypted using industry-standard encryption protocols (e.g., TLS) during transmission.
- The system will comply with relevant financial regulations and standards, such as PCI DSS for handling credit card information.
- User authentication will be handled through a secure login system, potentially incorporating multi-factor authentication (MFA).

Failure Modes:
- If the payment gateway is unavailable, the system should queue the transaction and retry processing at regular intervals.
- In case of invalid payment details, the system should reject the transaction and prompt the user to correct the information.
- If authentication fails, the system should deny access and prompt the user to retry or reset their credentials.
- The system should log all failed transactions for audit and troubleshooting purposes.

Edge Cases:
- Handling of payments with zero or negative amounts should be explicitly rejected.
- Transactions involving currency conversion should account for exchange rates and potential fees.
- The system should handle duplicate transaction attempts gracefully, ensuring that users are not charged multiple times for the same transaction.

Security Constraints:
- All sensitive payment information must be encrypted both in transit and at rest.
- The system should implement access controls to restrict who can view or modify payment data.
- Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments should be conducted to identify and mitigate potential threats.
- The system should support secure logging and monitoring to detect and respond to suspicious activities.

Performance Constraints:
- The system should process payments within a reasonable time frame, ideally under 5 seconds per transaction.
- It should be capable of handling peak loads, such as during sales events, without degradation in performance.
- The system should be scalable to accommodate increasing transaction volumes as the user base grows.
